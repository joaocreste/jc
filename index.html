Profile

An unusual intersection

I am an Italian-Brazilian engineer educated in France and Brazil, currently completing an MBA at ESCP Business School across Paris and London. Raised in a family of academics, I have always been drawn to first-principles thinking — the habit of understanding not just what is happening, but why and what it implies.

My career has followed an unusual arc: from electrical engineering and applied research, to M&A and greenfield development in renewable energy, to equity research covering high-growth technology companies, to portfolio management at a multi-family office. Each chapter has been different in function, but unified by a consistent pull toward the same three domains — energy, finance, and the analytical tools that connect them.

I am convinced that the energy transition is the defining challenge of our generation, and that it cannot be solved by engineers or financiers working in isolation. What it demands is people who understand both the technical complexity of energy systems and the financial logic that drives capital allocation — and who can harness data and AI to make better decisions under uncertainty. That convergence is where I want to operate.

Outside of work, I compose music — eight years of conservatory-level piano studies. I also build tools: Forager, an interactive mapping platform for solar, wind, and infrastructure assets, started as a side project and has since become a useful real-world instrument.

Research Work

Academic research

Computer Vision · Robotics

Indoor Environment Mapping Using RGB-D Cameras

RGB-D cameras are systems capable of capturing RGB images along with distance measurements, pixel by pixel, and can be used to build dense 3D maps. The goal of the project was to study the use of these cameras in indoor environment mapping by robots, in order to develop a system that autonomously creates a detailed map of the studied area. Frame alignment algorithms for environment mapping were evaluated, with special attention to their accuracy and efficiency in predetermined scenarios.

Reinforcement Learning · Smart Grid

Coordination of Electric Vehicle Charging through Multiagent Reinforcement Learning

The increasing adoption of plug-in electric vehicles (PEVs) presents significant challenges to residential power distribution grids, including transformer overloads and undesirable demand peaks caused by uncoordinated home charging. This work proposed MASCO (MultiAgent Selfish-COllaborative architecture), a distributed multiagent reinforcement learning framework for coordinating PEV charging in residential neighborhoods. Each agent autonomously learns both a selfish policy — optimising its own objectives such as minimising electricity costs and maintaining battery charge — and collaborative policies that account for the goals of neighbouring agents. A cooperation criterion based on W-Learning dynamically arbitrates between selfish and collaborative behaviour at each decision step. Experiments simulating a 15-house neighbourhood demonstrated that MASCO significantly reduced daily transformer overloads compared to an always-charging baseline, while maintaining battery charge levels above 99.9% in most configurations.

What I care about

Three domains, one thesis

I
Energy Transition

The energy transition is the defining infrastructure challenge of our generation — a full rewiring of how the world produces and consumes energy. I have worked across its value chain: closing renewable energy M&A in Brazil, assessing green hydrogen feasibility, and studying the capital structures that make large-scale clean energy projects bankable.

II
Technology & AI

I covered 30+ high-growth technology companies as an equity analyst, and I have built data tools — from GIS mapping platforms to automated fund screening systems — that convert raw information into decisions. AI will not replace decision-makers, but the decision-makers of tomorrow will be the ones who know how to work with it.

III
Finance & Capital Allocation

Capital determines which technologies get built and which transitions happen. I have been on both sides — allocating capital across global markets as a portfolio manager, and deploying it into real assets as an M&A practitioner. Finance is not a support function for the energy transition; it is a core driver of it.

To tackle the energy transition in a world shaped by AI, we must bring together technical and financial knowledge to make the right decisions. Will the decision-makers be AIs? No — but the people behind them will be the ones who understand both the complexity of energy systems and the logic of capital. That is the convergence I am building toward.
