Profile

An unusual intersection

I am an Italian-Brazilian engineer educated in France and Brazil, currently completing an MBA at ESCP Business School across Paris and London. Raised in a family of academics, I have always been drawn to first-principles thinking — the habit of understanding not just what is happening, but why and what it implies.

My career has followed an unusual arc: from electrical engineering and applied research, to M&A and greenfield development in renewable energy, to equity research in global technology markets, to portfolio management at a multi-family office, and now to entrepreneurial ventures in green hydrogen. Each chapter has been different in sector and function, but unified by a consistent drive — to operate at the edge where technical depth meets strategic judgment.

I am drawn to private equity in infrastructure and energy transition because it is a craft that rewards both rigour and imagination. I want to build things that last: portfolios, platforms, and eventually ventures of my own in the energy space.

Outside of work, I compose music — eight years of conservatory-level piano studies. I also build tools: Forager, an interactive mapping platform for solar, wind, and infrastructure assets, started as a side project and has since become a useful real-world instrument.

Transaction Overview

Terra Santa was Total Eren's first wind asset in Brazil, developed in partnership with Vila Energia Renovável. Structured with a bilateral 20-year private PPA with Cemig GT — a creditworthy state-owned offtaker — the project provided long-term revenue visibility for project finance. Construction began late 2019; commercial operation commenced February 2022, marking a pivotal diversification of Total Eren's Brazilian portfolio beyond solar.

My Role

  • Originated the opportunity within the 50+ GW pipeline database I built for Total Eren Brazil and recommended the acquisition to Paris.
  • Led bilateral PPA negotiation and all transaction documentation from NBO through binding MOU to shareholders' and shares purchase agreements.
  • Coordinated due diligence workstreams with legal, technical, and financial advisors across French and Brazilian teams.
  • Represented Total Eren in partner negotiations with Vila Energia to establish the 90/10 joint ownership structure.

Transaction Overview

The Maral acquisition involved the purchase of two wind project SPEs from the Brazilian unit of Voltalia SA, a publicly listed French renewable energy developer. At the time of acquisition both projects had yet to break ground — a pre-construction buy-side deal requiring robust technical and regulatory diligence. Cleared without restrictions by CADE. Both phases entered operation in 2021 under a 20-year private PPA with Cemig GT, Total Eren holding 100% ownership.

My Role

  • Identified Maral within the proprietary pipeline database and recommended it to Paris as a strategic pre-construction entry into wind.
  • Led buy-side negotiation with Voltalia Brazil from initial NBO through final shares purchase and shareholders' agreements.
  • Managed CADE regulatory filing coordination with external legal counsel, ensuring a clean unconditional clearance.
  • Structured the 100% ownership approach, assessing and rejecting partnership options to maintain full control for Total Eren.

Transaction Overview

Dracena is a 90 MW solar photovoltaic complex comprising three 30 MW units in São Paulo State — one of Total Eren's foundational Brazilian assets and the platform from which wind expansion was pursued. The project secured a 20-year PPA through Brazil's federal reserve energy auction (ANEEL, 2014). Project finance achieved via BRL 280M in long-term infrastructure debentures subscribed in full by Kinea Investimentos, with BNP and XP Investimentos as financial advisors.

My Role

  • Supported business development and project delivery during construction and ramp-up, working directly under the Managing Director of Total Eren Brazil.
  • Contributed to the internal project database that benchmarked Dracena against the broader solar pipeline, informing capital allocation decisions.
  • Coordinated with local teams, contractors, and regulators during the transition to commercial operation, tracking KPIs and reporting to Paris headquarters.
